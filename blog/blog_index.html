Stereotype Threat

How "bad-vibes" actually bring us down

A fascinating new field in social psychology is the "Stereotype Threat." Pioneered by Claude Steele, stereotype threat refers to the phenomenon of stereotyped individuals actually conforming to the stereotype they belong to. The data is scary, because stereotype threats exist everywhere, and we are all constantly battling around them. It shows us just how influenced we are by experiences around us, and how much our own psychology depends on our perception of ourselves.
